# Platforms and Release Footprint

## Original launch

- **Arcade (1982)** as the lead platform and core business driver.

## Home and later platform footprint (representative list)

> Note: release naming/variants differ by region and era (direct port vs. compilation appearance).

- Atari 2600
- Atari 5200
- Atari 8-bit computers
- Commodore 64
- VIC-20
- Apple II
- IBM PC / DOS-era environments
- NES/Famicom-era through related releases/collections
- Game Boy / Game Boy Color contexts
- SNES/Genesis era via collections and legacy packages
- PlayStation era collections
- Xbox era collections
- Nintendo DS / Wii / later Nintendo digital channels
- Mobile legacy releases/collections
- Plug-and-play retro devices

## Platform strategy insight

The strategy was not “one platform, one sale.” It was:

- monetize in arcades first,
- then repeatedly capture new cohorts through ports/compilations,
- sustaining lifetime franchise awareness far beyond initial cabinet sales.

## Presentation tip

Use a single slide with a horizontal timeline (1982 → present) and logos grouped by decade. That visual quickly demonstrates long-tail monetization.
